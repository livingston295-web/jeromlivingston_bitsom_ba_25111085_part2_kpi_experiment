# Hypothesis Test Notes

## Business Objective

Determine whether the new onboarding campaign (Treatment group) improves the Paid Conversion Rate compared to the existing onboarding experience (Control group).

## Null Hypothesis (H0)

There is no significant difference in Paid Conversion Rate between the Control group and the Treatment group.

## Alternative Hypothesis (H1)

The Treatment group has a higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

## Test Type

One-tailed comparison of Control vs Treatment.

## Significance Level

5% (0.05)

## Primary Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

## Decision Rule

If the Treatment group's Paid Conversion Rate is higher than the Control group's Paid Conversion Rate and the statistical evidence supports the improvement, the campaign can be considered for launch.

## Business Interpretation

The experiment compares the existing onboarding experience with the new onboarding campaign to determine whether the new experience increases user conversion while maintaining acceptable business performance.

## Conclusion

The final recommendation should consider both the Paid Conversion Rate and the guardrail metrics such as Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, and Average Days to Convert before making a launch decision.
